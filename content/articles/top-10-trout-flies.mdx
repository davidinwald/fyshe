# Top 10 Trout Flies Every Angler Should Carry

If you could only carry ten fly patterns, these would cover nearly every situation you'll encounter on a trout stream.

## 1. Adams (#12-20)

The Adams is the most versatile dry fly ever tied. Its gray body and grizzly hackle suggest a wide range of mayflies and can be fished effectively year-round. When in doubt, tie on an Adams.

**Best for**: General purpose dry fly fishing, mayfly hatches

## 2. Elk Hair Caddis (#12-18)

This buoyant, easy-to-see pattern imitates adult caddisflies. Its elk hair wing keeps it floating high even in rough water. An essential pattern from spring through fall.

**Best for**: Caddis hatches, riffles, pocket water

## 3. Pheasant Tail Nymph (#14-20)

The Pheasant Tail is probably the most effective nymph pattern in the world. Its slim profile and natural coloring imitate a wide range of mayfly nymphs. Fish it dead-drift near the bottom.

**Best for**: Year-round nymphing, mayfly nymph imitation

## 4. Woolly Bugger (#6-12)

Part streamer, part leech, part everything — the Woolly Bugger catches every species of fish that swims. Tie it in black, olive, and brown. Strip it, swing it, or dead-drift it.

**Best for**: Streamers, searching patterns, big fish

## 5. Gold Ribbed Hare's Ear (#12-18)

The GRHE is the ultimate "buggy" nymph. Its rough dubbing and gold rib suggest dozens of aquatic insects. A must-have for any nymph box.

**Best for**: General purpose nymphing, stonefly and mayfly imitation

## 6. Copper John (#14-20)

John Barr's Copper John sinks fast and triggers aggressive strikes. The copper wire body catches light underwater, and the bead head gets it down quickly.

**Best for**: Deep nymphing, fast water, drop-shot rigs

## 7. Parachute Adams (#14-20)

The parachute version of the Adams sits lower in the film and is easier to see thanks to the white post. Many anglers prefer it over the traditional Adams.

**Best for**: Flat water, selective trout, emerger situations

## 8. Zebra Midge (#18-24)

Small but deadly. Midges hatch year-round, and trout feed on them more than any other insect. A simple thread body with a bead head is all you need.

**Best for**: Winter fishing, tailwaters, spring creeks

## 9. Stimulator (#8-14)

The Stimulator is a big, visible attractor that works as a stonefly imitation and a general searching pattern. It's also an excellent indicator fly for a dry-dropper rig.

**Best for**: Summer fishing, stonefly hatches, dry-dropper rigs

## 10. San Juan Worm (#10-14)

Love it or hate it, the San Juan Worm catches fish. Tied in red, wine, or tan, it imitates aquatic worms that trout feed on regularly — especially after rain.

**Best for**: High water, runoff, year-round

## Building Your Fly Box

Start with sizes 14-16 for most patterns. Carry a range from #12 down to #20 as you build your collection. Focus on having the right size and silhouette rather than an exact color match — presentation matters more than perfection.
