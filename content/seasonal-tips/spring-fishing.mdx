# Spring Fishing Tips

Spring is arguably the best time to fish. Water temperatures are rising, insects are hatching, and fish are actively feeding after a long winter.

## Early Spring (Water 40-50F)

- Fish are sluggish — use slow presentations
- Nymphs and streamers work best
- Focus on the warmest part of the day (noon to 3pm)
- Look for dark-bottomed pools that warm faster
- Small midges (#20-24) can produce when nothing else works

## Mid Spring (Water 50-60F)

- **The magic window**. Fish are active and feeding aggressively
- Blue Wing Olive hatches begin — carry BWO dries (#16-20) and emergers
- Streamers remain effective, especially on cloudy days
- March Brown and Hendrickson mayflies start appearing
- Caddis start their annual emergence

## Late Spring (Water 55-65F)

- Peak dry fly season begins
- Caddis hatches are in full swing — Elk Hair Caddis (#14-16)
- Stonefly hatches on larger rivers — Stimulators (#8-12)
- Evening spinner falls provide incredible fishing
- Terrestrials start becoming relevant on warm days

## General Spring Tips

1. **Watch the water temperature**. A few degrees can make all the difference
2. **Fish after rain** — runoff washes worms and insects into the water
3. **Don't overlook small streams** — they warm faster and fish activate sooner
4. **Adjust your depth** — as water warms, fish move from deep holds to shallower feeding lanes
5. **Carry emerger patterns** — spring hatches often keep fish feeding just below the surface

## Spring Fly Box Essentials

- Blue Wing Olive (#16-20)
- Pheasant Tail Nymph (#14-18)
- Elk Hair Caddis (#14-16)
- Woolly Bugger, olive (#8-10)
- Zebra Midge (#18-22)
- March Brown (#12-14)
- San Juan Worm (#12)
